Ingredients

~ 1 cup lentils
~ 4 Bratwurst (do NOT substitute with franks; the higher quality the better)
~ 2 garlic cloves
~ "Zest and Juice of 1 Lemon" (for simplicity: 2-4 tablespoons lemon juice)
~ Plenty of kosher salt
~ A Vegetable (cauliflower, shredded spinach [what I used], broccoli)
Ã‰pices (du Mojo): 1/3 cup Olive Oil, generous Rosemary, 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (can substitute with 1/8 teaspoon of cayenne, paprika, and chile powder each)

Recipe

  1. Cook lentils in simmering salted water (in a saucepan/pot), 20-25 minutes, then drain (do other steps as this is happening)
  2. Place generous rosemary, garlic, and pepper in a bowl
  3. Heat 1/3 cup olive oil in a skillet until shimmering, AKA incredibly hot [tip: if oil starts smoking it's good], and pour over aromatics in bowl, letting sit for 10 minutes before stirring in the lemon zest/juice and some salt (this is the mojo).
  4. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in skillet, and cook Bratwursts until deeply browned and releasing some fat, about 7-9 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
  5. Cook the lentils in the skillet on high heat for about 5+ minutes until crispy as desired
  6. Put lentils in the pot, add vegetable to the mojo, and add to lentils. Add more salt and oil to taste.
  7. Serve: Divide lentils amongst plates, and then put bratwurst on top.
